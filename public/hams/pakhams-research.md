# PAKHAMS / PARS SWL and HAM Registration Research

Collected from official PAKHAMS/PARS pages and official PDFs on 2026-05-20.

## What PAKHAMS / PARS Is

Pakistan Amateur Radio Society (PARS), also branded online as PAKHAMS, describes itself as a national charitable non-profit organization for amateur radio enthusiasts in Pakistan. It operates a QSL bureau, supports awards and contests, represents Pakistani amateur radio operators before national and international regulatory authorities, and is Pakistan's national member society in the International Amateur Radio Union.

Official site: https://pakhams.com/

## SWL vs HAM

An SWL is a Short Wave Listener. In the PAKHAMS process, SWL membership is the entry stage before applying to PTA for an Amateur Radio License. The official Become Member page says a person must first acquire PARS membership as SWL to start the process for obtaining an Amateur Radio License from PTA.

A HAM / amateur radio operator is a licensed operator allowed to legally operate amateur radio equipment. PAKHAMS describes amateur radio as a technical service/hobby used for self-training, communication, experimentation, satellites, voice, Morse code, computer messages, emergency communication, awards, and contests. PTA licensing is required before legal operation.

Important legal difference: SWL membership does not authorize transmitting. The 2026 SWL affidavit explicitly says the applicant will not transmit on any frequency without the required PTA license.

## Registration Path

### Step 1: Become a PARS SWL Member

The official PAKHAMS page says the first step is PARS membership as Short Wave Listener (SWL). The current uploaded 2026 SWL form asks for:

- Name and father's name
- Mailing address and post code
- CNIC number
- Date of birth
- Email
- Profession
- Qualification
- License number/date of issue, if already licensed
- Phone and mobile
- Applicant signature and date

The 2026 SWL form requirements are:

- Fill the form in clear block letters
- Minimum age for SWL membership: 12 years
- Copy of NADRA card or B-form
- One attested passport-size photograph
- Attested copies of academic and/or technical qualifications
- Affidavit on stamp paper using the provided format
- Reference of one HAM member, or character certificate from an educational institution for students, or certificate from a Grade 17+ gazetted officer for other cases
- Fee receipt attached

2026 SWL membership fees listed by the form:

- Students: PKR 3,000 for three years
- Other SWL cases: PKR 4,500 for three years
- Licensed amateur membership: PKR 2,000 per year
- Account: UBL 0659065901007917, Pakistan Amateur Radio Society

### Step 2: Apply to PTA for Amateur Radio License

The PAKHAMS Become Member page links to PTA amateur license downloads and says a PTA test and interview will be conducted. The page says the applicant must submit two sets/triplicate-style documentation for the license proposal.

The PAKHAMS page lists these documents:

1. Prescribed Application Form
2. Affidavit prescribed in the PARS membership form
3. Letter of intent
4. National Identity Card
5. Two passport-size photographs
6. List of equipment including layout sketch of the antenna
7. Sketch of exact geographical location so PTA inspector can approach the station
8. Any other certificate/testimonial supporting the license case
9. Certificate of clearance of antecedents from local police
10. Proof of membership of Pakistan Amateur Radio Society (PARS)

The PTA citizen application PDF says documents must be attached in duplicate:

- Attested CNIC/Form-B copy
- Attested copies of two recent passport-size photographs
- Police Character Certificate
- For minors: supervisor name, CNIC, amateur radio license number, and consent
- Equipment list with antenna layout sketch
- Proof of one year PARS SWL membership for age 18 and above
- Proof of two years SWL for age 12 and below 18 years
- Sketch of exact station location
- Other supporting certificates/testimonials

### Step 3: PTA Test, Interview, Security Clearance, Fee, and License

PTA SOP process:

- Application is submitted to Director (RBS), PTA HQs, in two complete sets.
- PTA checks deficiencies; if complete, the case is forwarded for LEA security clearance.
- Applicant is informed of written test and interview date.
- PTA allows a maximum of three attempts; after three failures the applicant must re-apply.
- LEAs are expected to respond within 12 weeks.
- If the applicant passes written test/interview, PTA issues a demand note for fee deposit.
- After payment and approval, provisional permission can be issued.
- On LEA clearance, PTA prepares and prints the Amateur License Card.

PTA SOP fees:

- Examination fee: PKR 100
- Initial license fee: PKR 1,000 for five years
- Renewal fee: PKR 1,000 for five years
- Change of location: PKR 200
- Duplicate license: PKR 200

Note: the PAKHAMS visible page says PTA will issue a demand note of Rs. 450 for processing plus five-year license fee, but the PTA SOP linked by PAKHAMS states Rs. 100 examination fee and Rs. 1,000 initial license fee for five years. For current implementation copy, treat the PTA SOP as the stronger license-fee source and preserve the PAKHAMS page wording as historical/page content.

## Eligibility Details

PAKHAMS visible page says an individual applicant must:

- Be a Pakistani citizen
- Be not less than 18 years of age
- Be able to communicate in English
- Have SWL membership of PARS

PTA SOP linked by PAKHAMS says individuals must:

- Be a Pakistani citizen
- Be not less than 16 years of age
- Be able to communicate in English
- Qualify the prescribed written test and interview
- Be an SWL of PARS or another acceptable Pakistan radio club for at least one year if 18 or above, or at least two years if 16 to under 18
- Be able to present a significant number of QSL acknowledgement cards

The 2026 SWL form says minimum SWL age is 12 years. The PTA citizen form references SWL proof for age 12 and below 18. There is therefore an official-source mismatch between the web page and the linked/current forms. Keep the distinction clear in website copy.

## PTA Test Syllabus

The PTA syllabus PDF says the written test has two parts:

- Technical knowledge: elementary electricity, magnetism, radio theory and practice
- Operational knowledge: amateur license SOP, ITU radio regulations related to Amateur Service, Q codes, phonetic alphabet, abbreviations, Morse code conventions

Technical topics include:

- Static charge, AC/DC current, conductors, insulators, Ohm's law, amperes, watts, inductance, capacitance
- Audio/radio frequency bands, resonance, transformers
- Receiver/transmitter circuits, sensitivity, selectivity, tone, cross modulation, squelch, signal-to-noise ratio, beat frequency oscillator
- RF spectrum, oscillator types, modulation modes, propagation, ground waves, ionospheric layers, sky waves, skip zone, fading
- Antenna types/construction/characteristics
- Measuring instruments
- Elementary space communication

Operational topics include:

- PTA, PARS, ITU, IARU and amateur societies
- Pakistan Amateur Service Regulations, 2004
- Frequency bands, callsigns, interference, distress/emergency transmissions
- International time and frequency standards
- Q codes including QRA, QRG, QRH, QRJ, QRK, QRL, QRM, QRN, QRS, QRT, QRV, QRX, QRZ, QSB, QSL, QSO, QSP, QSY, QTH, QTR
- Phonetic alphabet

The syllabus says applicants with recognized university degrees/diplomas in electrical, electronics, and communications may be exempted from the technical part, but still have to answer operational aspects.

## Operating Rules and Limits

The PTA Terms and Conditions PDF says:

- Amateur stations are for self-training in wireless communication techniques, with personal aim and no pecuniary interest.
- Stations must only transmit/receive on specified amateur bands.
- The station cannot be operated by anyone except the licensed person.
- Conversation is restricted mainly to wireless equipment, radio propagation reports, signal reports, personal family matters, and weather.
- Politics, religion, controversial subjects, music, taped broadcast material, misleading calls/signals/news, advertisements, business matters, third-party commercial messages, and offensive/racial/religious/communal content are prohibited.
- In emergencies and natural calamities, the licensee and equipment can be called upon by PTA or the Federal Government to help provide communication.
- A station logbook must be maintained and available for inspection.
- Mobile or portable operation is generally not allowed except with special PTA authorization.
